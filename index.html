Hi! I'm Kathryn, but I go by Katie :)

Originally from Vancouver, WA, I am now enjoying life in Seattle after getting my undergraduate degree at the University of Washington in Computer Science.

My interests in CS encompass human-computer interaction, accessibility, education, and programming languages. I love these areas and especially the opportunities when two or more areas intersect.

Work

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant, UW CSE (Sep 2020 - Mar 2021)

  • Taught material for an introductory programming languages course and an intermediate programming class, supporting students through mind-bending topics like functional programming and assembly.
  • Cultivating community in the CS department through encouragement in section and office hours, creating welcoming spaces to build relationships during a virtual quarter.
  • Students gave responses about how phrases like "I couldn't have explained that better" and slow explanations helped develop a nonjudgemental learning environment. It reminded me of how inclusive learning experiences are often the most impactful.
  • Feedback from my professor helped me realize communication areas to improve upon and motivated me to maintain my devotion to thorough preparation.

Software Development Engineer Intern, Expedia Group (Jun 2019 - Aug 2020)

  • Collaborated between teams and business areas to design a recommentation microservice for travelers.
  • Combined content filtering and collaborative filtering with AWS Elasticsearch to tune a personalized recommendation system.
  • Worked in a two week sprint cadence and learned AGILE through practice.
  • Participated in Expedia's Ability Inclusion Movement (AIM) and became involved with mental health leaders and events in Expedia. Through these experiences, I witnessed how accessibility, diversity, and tech could overlap in the same space in industry.

Undergraduate Research Assistant, UW Make4All Group (Jan 2019 - Aug 2020)

  • Tactile Maps Project: Created a system for visually impaired individuals to create custom 3D printed interactive maps. Used the Open Street Maps Database and Autodesk Fusion 360 Python API.
  • Accessible Knitting Project: Exploring accessibility needs surrounding knitting through qualitative analysis of interviews.
  • In addition to learning how inaccessible the world around me is, I learned what a truly inclusive lab feels like. Despite inexperience, I received mentorship and support that helped me grow as a developer, team member, and researcher to acheive goals of leading interviews, analyzing data, and dabbling in fabrication.

Information Technology Intern, Korry Electronics (Jun 2019 - Sep 2019)

  • Designed and implemented intranet structure, content, and permissions with DNN Software, HTML/CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Performed a gap analysis of the current and new intranet by meeting with department managers and led technical training for web administrators.
  • Even given the content constraints from department managers, I had design freedom with UX, security measures, and passing off the project after the internship. I had a lot of fun with designing webpage structure, web admin roles, and simplified instruction videos for website maintenance in the future.
  • My biggest takeaways were the soft skills I'd built and practice I had aligning my technical tasks with business goals.
